# The Memory Labyrinth: Genealogy of Fear and the Bureaucracy of Terror

## Project Description

**The Memory Labyrinth: Genealogy of Fear and the Bureaucracy of Terror** is an experimental interactive narrative created with the Twine platform. The project explores the transmission of historical trauma, the role of archival documents, and the mechanisms of bureaucratic power in shaping personal and collective memory.

The interactive experience invites the player to navigate a layered narrative that moves between family memory, archival evidence, and institutional procedures. Through this journey, the project reflects on how silence, fear, and administrative systems influence the way historical events are remembered and interpreted.

The project combines elements of digital storytelling, archival research, and narrative game design to create an immersive environment where the player engages directly with fragments of memory and historical documentation.

## Narrative Structure

The narrative is structured in three main parts:

1. **The Wall of Silence**
   A conversation with a grandfather whose memory is shaped by inherited narratives and protective clichés.

2. **Encounter with the Archive**
   The player examines digitized archival documents that reveal fragments of the past while also exposing the limits of bureaucratic records.

3. **The Ritual of Submission**
   A reconstruction of a bureaucratic procedure in which the descendant of a repressed individual must compose a carefully worded petition for rehabilitation.

These sections reflect different modes of remembering: oral memory, documentary evidence, and bureaucratic language.
